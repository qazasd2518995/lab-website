One-way ANOVA

單因子變異數分析 · partitioning variance into signal and noise

Overview

With three or more groups, ANOVA splits the total variance into a between-groups part (differences among group means) and a within-groups part (noise inside each group). Their ratio of mean squares is the F-statistic. A large F says the between-group signal dwarfs the within-group noise.

Key formulas
$$SS_{\text{total}} = SS_{\text{between}} + SS_{\text{within}}$$
$$F = \dfrac{MS_{\text{between}}}{MS_{\text{within}}}, \quad MS = \dfrac{SS}{df}$$
$$df_{\text{between}} = k - 1, \quad df_{\text{within}} = N - k$$
$$\eta^2 = \dfrac{SS_{\text{between}}}{SS_{\text{total}}}$$
Variance, partitioned
Worked example · three teaching methods

Three groups of n = 5 learners, vocabulary post-test scores. The total sum of squares (140) splits into 90 between groups and 50 within. Dividing each by its degrees of freedom gives the mean squares, and their ratio is F.

The F-distribution & its critical value

Under H₀ the F-statistic follows an F-distribution with (dfbetween, dfwithin) degrees of freedom. Reject when the observed F lands in the upper tail beyond the critical value.

Try it: the F-ratio simulator

Drag the mean squares and watch F move. When the between-groups mean square is large relative to within-groups, F clears its critical value and the test turns significant.

Interactive F critical-value table

Pick a significance level, then read the critical F at the intersection of numerator df (between) and denominator df (within). Type any df pair for an exact value.

After a significant F: post-hoc & effect size

A significant omnibus F says some means differ, not which. Tukey HSD compares every pair while holding the family-wise error rate at α. For effect size, η² is simple but upward-biased; ω² corrects that bias and is the better population estimate.

$$\text{HSD} = q_{\alpha,\,k,\,df_w}\sqrt{\dfrac{MS_{\text{within}}}{n}}$$
$$\omega^2 = \dfrac{SS_{\text{between}} - (k-1)\,MS_{\text{within}}}{SS_{\text{total}} + MS_{\text{within}}}$$
Assumptions
  • Independent observations within and across groups.
  • Normality of residuals in each group.
  • Homogeneity of variance (check with Levene's test; if it fails, use Welch's ANOVA and Games–Howell).
When to use
  • Comparing means across three or more independent groups.
  • Quantifying effect size with η² or ω² after a significant F.
  • Following up with post-hoc tests (e.g. Tukey HSD).
