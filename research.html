Research Foci

Six methodological vantage points.

Each focus is a different restriction of the master operator, a particular configuration of Λ, Z, and ∂/∂t that admits a tractable estimator and a substantive question. The methods are compositional.

Estimation of latent constructs and their structural relations across nested populations, with simultaneous decomposition of within- and between-level covariance structure.

Random-coefficient and growth-curve specifications for clustered and longitudinal data. Cross-classified and multiple-membership designs. Bayesian estimation for sparse upper-level units.

IRT-based item selection, exposure control, and content balancing. Multidimensional CAT, cognitive diagnostic models, and the calibration of large operational item banks.

Meta-Analysis

Random-effects synthesis, network and dose-response models, robust variance estimation, and meta-analytic SEM. We aggregate evidence across studies while honoring its heterogeneity.

— 05 / VI

Multivariate Data Mining

Pattern discovery in high-dimensional behavioral, educational, and psychometric data through penalized methods, manifold learning, and ensemble inference.

The mathematical substrate of the lab. We derive new estimators, sensitivity and information matrices, and dynamic-systems formulations of change processes.

§ 04
Methodological Capabilities

A working catalogue of operations the lab performs.

From variance decomposition to dynamical systems, these are the recurring techniques the lab brings to applied problems.

i.
Variance decomposition
Partitioning observed variance into latent, level-specific, and residual components across nested designs.
ML-SEM · HLM
ii.
Latent trait recovery
Item response theory, factor-analytic, and mixture models for unobserved person and item parameters.
IRT · CAT
iii.
Adaptive measurement
Item selection algorithms maximizing information at provisional ability estimates; multidimensional and constraint-aware extensions.
CAT · MIRT
iv.
Evidence synthesis
Random-effects, network, and three-level meta-analytic models with robust variance estimation and moderator analysis.
META
v.
High-dimensional inference
Regularization, dimension reduction, and ensemble methods adapted to psychometric and behavioral data structures.
DATA MINING
vi.
Analytic derivation
Closed-form gradients, Hessians, and Fisher information for new estimators via matrix calculus.
MATRIX
vii.
Dynamical systems
Continuous-time models of change, growth, and coupling formulated as differential or stochastic differential equations.
ODE · SDE
