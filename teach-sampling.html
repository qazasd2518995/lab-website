Sampling distribution & standard error

抽樣分配與標準誤 · the two properties that make inference possible

Overview

Enumerate every possible sample of a tiny population and the sample mean reveals two exact properties. It is unbiased: averaged over all samples it equals the population mean. And its spread shrinks with sample size as variance reduction. Together they underwrite every confidence interval and every test statistic.

Key formulas
$$E(\bar{X}) = \mu$$
$$\mathrm{Var}(\bar{X}) = \dfrac{\sigma^2}{n}$$
$$\mathrm{SE}(\bar{X}) = \dfrac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$
See it: all nine samples

Take the population {1, 2, 3} and draw every sample of size n = 2 with replacement. There are nine equally likely samples. Their means cluster around the population mean μ = 2, and the mean of those means is exactly 2 — that is what unbiased means, made concrete.

The √n law — and its cost

Because the standard error falls as σ/√n, precision improves only with the square root of sample size. Halving the standard error does not take twice the data — it takes four times. The table below holds σ = 12 fixed and shows what each sample size buys.
