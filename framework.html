The Unifying Framework

One operator equation. Three axes of decomposition.

Every method this lab deploys is a particular instance of a single mathematical object: a composed operator that maps latent structure to observed data through three independent axes of variation: measurement, hierarchy, and dynamics. The methodological diversity below is not a list of techniques. It is a coordinate system.

y  =  𝓜[θ]  x  +  ε
where    𝓜  =  𝓛(Λ)  ∘  𝓗(Z, G)  ∘  𝓓(∂/∂t)
yobserved multivariate response: vector, matrix, or tensor of measurements xdesign or predictor structure governing input 𝓛measurement operator with loadings Λ; projects latent η onto observables 𝓗hierarchical operator with design Z and variance components G, R; propagates nesting 𝓓dynamical operator (∂/∂t, ∫dt, stochastic dW); governs change and flow θfull parameter vector subject to estimation by matrix-calculus derivatives
— AXIS I
Λ

Measurement / Dimensionality

The projection from latent η onto observed y. Determines how many constructs the data express, and how variance distributes across them. Multivariate by default, tensor-valued when needed.

— Canonical form y = Λη + ε
— AXIS II
Z

Hierarchy / Nesting

The architecture of clustering. Within-cluster covariance R and between-cluster covariance G are propagated through design matrix Z to yield the marginal variance of the response.

— Canonical form V = ZGZ⊤ + R
— AXIS III
∂/∂t

Dynamics / Time

The temporal evolution operator. Encodes change, growth, coupling, and stochastic perturbation through ordinary or stochastic differential equations on the latent state.

— Canonical form dη = Aη dt + σ dW
